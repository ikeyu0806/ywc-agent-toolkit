# Market Timing and Feature Prioritization Checklist

Assess the service's positioning relative to market trends, competitor expectations, and opportunity windows.

## Core Questions

1. **Market trends alignment**
   - What current trends does this service benefit from?
   - Are there emerging trends it could capitalize on but hasn't addressed?
   - Is the service ahead of, aligned with, or behind market expectations?

2. **Competitive gaps**
   - What capabilities do comparable services offer that this one lacks?
   - What does this service do that competitors don't — is that advantage maintained?
   - Are there table-stakes features expected by users that are missing?

3. **Feature prioritization**
   - Based on user value and growth mechanics, what is the highest-impact next feature to build?
   - Are there low-effort, high-impact improvements being overlooked?
   - Are current development priorities aligned with user needs and market opportunity?

4. **Timing-sensitive opportunities**
   - Are there market windows that are open now but may close soon?
   - Are there platform or ecosystem shifts the service should prepare for?
   - Are there regulatory or industry changes that create opportunity or risk?

5. **Differentiation sustainability**
   - Is the service's competitive advantage durable, or could a competitor replicate it quickly?
   - Are there network effects, data advantages, or switching costs protecting the position?
   - Is the moat growing or shrinking over time?

## Priority Signals

Raise to **High** if:
- A table-stakes feature expected by users in this category is missing
- A market window is closing and the service is not yet positioned to capture it
- The service's differentiation is thin and easily replicated

Raise to **Medium** if:
- Market trend alignment is weak but not immediately critical
- Competitive gaps exist in secondary (not core) features

Raise to **Low** if:
- Positioning is strong but could be further reinforced over time
